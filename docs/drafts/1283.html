Someone tweeted a link to this, a podcast which discusses, among other things, a project that uses soccer balls to produce energy:

They came up with the idea for the sOccket, a soccer ball that generates and stores electricity during normal game play. The stored electricity in the ball can then be used to light an LED lamp, or charge a cellphone or battery.

“Soccer is something you will find in every African country,” one founder, Jessica Lin, told Green Inc. “People play for hours a day, so we thought, ‘Why not try to get a little more out of that energy?’ and that’s where the idea ultimately came from.”

[...]

For each 15 minutes of play, the ball can store enough energy to illuminate a small LED light for three hours, according to initial trials.

A bright LED typically consumes about 20 mA.  So that's 60 mAH. If you were to buy alkaline batteries in bulk, that would work out to about 1.5 cents' worth of power.  I don't know how much a charge-capable soccer ball would cost, but Amazon says that regular soccer balls cost about $10.  So that's 150 hours of playing soccer before we pay for a unit. Alternately, we could buy a hand-crank generator for about the same cost
